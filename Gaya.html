Part 1: Technical Specifications & Page Layout

1. What is the standard font family and size required for the main body text in a GAYA UKM thesis?

Explanation: Main narrative body text requires Times New Roman, size 12 pt.

2. Which of the following parameters is completely correct regarding the formatting of major chapter headings (e.g., BAB I / CHAPTER I)?

Explanation: Under GAYA UKM, major chapter titles must be bold, written completely in uppercase (CAPITALS), centered, and reduced to 11 pt.

3. What is the specific line spacing and alignment combination mandated for the main text body?

Explanation: Text blocks must use 1.5 line spacing and be fully justified on the page.

4. How should the first paragraph immediately below any heading or subheading be structured regarding its indentation?

Explanation: The absolute first paragraph after any heading starts completely flush left without any indentation.

5. For subsequent paragraphs (excluding the first one under a heading), what is the required indentation for the first line?

Explanation: Second and all subsequent consecutive paragraphs must have their first line indented by exactly 12.7 mm (0.5 inches).

6. What is the specific top margin measurement for pages hosting a brand-new chapter heading?

Explanation: Regular pages have a 30 mm top margin, but pages starting a new chapter have a deep drop margin of 50 mm from the top edge.

7. Which page margin requires a setting of 38 mm to guarantee text safety during physical bookbinding?

Explanation: The left margin is designated at 38 mm for binding allowance. Top is 30 mm, bottom and right are 25 mm.

8. According to GAYA UKM rules, where must the page numbers be cleanly positioned?

Explanation: Page numbers reside in the top right corner, exactly 15 mm from the top edge. The last digit should sit 25 mm from the right boundary.

9. What numbering style must be allocated to all front matter pages (e.g., Acknowledgements, Table of Contents)?

Explanation: Front matter pages use small/lowercase Roman numerals. Arabic digits begin on page 1 of Chapter 1.

10. What is the maximum size boundary allowed for a Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) thesis at UKM?

Explanation: GAYA UKM sets a firm ceiling for PhD text layout at 100,000 words or 400 pages (whichever is reached first). Master's theses are limited to 60,000 words or 250 pages.
Part 2: In-Text Citation Formats

11. If the author's name is NOT structurally integrated into the text narrative, how must a single-author citation look inside the parenthetical brackets?

Explanation: A distinctive rule of GAYA UKM is the removal of the comma between the author's name and the year within parenthetical brackets. Thus, (Shaharir 2010) is correct.

12. When citing a single work co-authored by exactly two individuals inside parentheses, what punctuation/conjunction format is required?

Explanation: When placed completely within brackets, an ampersand (&) joins two co-authors without commas. If part of the narrative text outside brackets, "and" is written.

13. For a source written by three or more authors, how must the in-text citation be written starting from the very first mention?

Explanation: For three or more authors, GAYA UKM requires using the primary author followed immediately by "et al." even on the first citation instance. Note there is no comma before or after et al.

14. When multiple independent research papers are grouped into a single set of citation brackets, how must they be sorted and divided?

Explanation: Multiple independent entries must follow alphabetical sorting by author's name, with each entry split using a semicolon (e.g., (Abdul Samad 2011; Azahan 2011; Jamaluddin 2010)).

15. If you cite separate papers published across different years by the exact same author inside one bracket, how is it represented?

Explanation: When the author is identical, list the name once followed by the timeline sequence split cleanly by commas.

16. If a researcher publishes two distinct documents in the same calendar year (e.g., Sham in 2010), how are they identified in text?

Explanation: Add lower case alphabetical suffixes (a, b, c) attached cleanly to the year without spacing.

17. Which of the following shows the correct implementation for secondary source referencing (citing an author via someone else's work)?

Explanation: If writing in Malay, use "seperti yang dipetik oleh". If writing in English, use "as cited in". No commas are placed between names and years.

18. What string label is integrated when formatting a text citation for a document with no identified author or corporate owner?

Explanation: Set the acronym "Anon." (standing for Anonymous) directly in place of the author's name string.

19. If you are citing a personal communication or an interview that is not retrievable in public data collections, how should it be structured?

Explanation: Personal communications / communication records highlight the event details within the brackets but are omitted from the final reference list because they cannot be verified by readers.

20. For corporate or institutional authors (e.g., Department of Occupational Safety and Health), how is the first citation written?

Explanation: Spell out the entity name on first contact, followed immediately by the initialized acronym and year within the citation brackets. Subseqent references can use just the acronym.
Part 3: Reference List Formatting Constraints

21. What line spacing rule governs individual entries inside the final 'References' section?

Explanation: Unlike the body text which uses 1.5 line spacing, the reference list entries themselves must be single-spaced.

22. How should a Western author's name with a surname (e.g., Humphrey John Moule Bowen) be formatted in the reference list?

Explanation: Western names are inverted: Surname first, then a comma, followed immediately by initials without spaces between them (e.g., Bowen, H.J.M.).

23. Which element must be completely excluded when listing an author's name in a GAYA UKM reference entry?

Explanation: Academic and honorary titles are entirely removed. Prof. Dr. Shaharir Mohd Zain is written simply as Shaharir Mohd Zain.

24. For web resources, what parameter must be placed inside square brackets at the absolute end of the reference entry?

Explanation: Online sources require an explicit statement of the date you accessed the resource, contained in square brackets at the end.

25. What is the rule for spacing between individual entries in the final reference list?

Explanation: While each entry is single-spaced internally, a single-line space gap is required between distinct entries to maintain scannability.

26. Identify the precise punctuation error in this book reference entry based on GAYA UKM rules:
Miller, R.K., 2009. The Informed Argument. San Diego: Harcourt Brace.

Explanation: In GAYA UKM, there is no comma between the author block and the year. It should read: Miller, R.K. 2009.

27. In a journal reference entry, what do the values '72' and '113-123' stand for in this string: ...Research 72: 113-123.?

Explanation: The number immediately following the italicized journal name is the volume number, followed by a colon and the page range of the article.

28. How are Malay authors without a family surname handled in the final reference list?

Explanation: Malay names are not inverted. They are written in their natural order (e.g., Asmah Haji Omar is alphabetized under 'A').

29. When a journal article has an issue number in addition to a volume number, how is it represented under GAYA UKM guidelines?

Explanation: The issue number is enclosed in parentheses directly after the volume number, without spaces, followed by a colon and the pages: 72(4): 113-123.

30. What is the correct order of elements for an edited book compilation reference entry?

Explanation: For an edited book compilation, list the Editor(s), Year, Book Title (followed by Ed. for English or Peny. for Malay in parentheses), City of publication, and finally the Publisher name.
