9. In Cochran's mathematical formula for sample size calculation, what does the variable 'e' represent?

Explanation: In Cochran's equation, $e$ represents the desired level of statistical precision (the margin of allowable sampling error)[cite: 8].

10. When adjusting calculated base samples for the real world, why is a +30% buffer specifically recommended?

11. What are the four structural framework choices defined under Probability/Random Sampling?

12. If extracting a systematic random sample of 8 students from a total population pool of 40 students, what is your calculated skip interval?

Explanation: The skip interval $K$ is calculated by dividing the population by the sample size ($40 / 8 = 5$). Every 5th element is selected[cite: 8].

13. Which type of stratified sampling requires assigning distinct mathematical weights during analysis because sample size does NOT mirror population proportions?

Explanation: Disproportionate stratified sampling requires weights during evaluation because the subgroups are not sampled in proportion to their real-world population distribution[cite: 8].

14. What is the operational "Golden Rule" for achieving the best results within Cluster Random Sampling?

Explanation: For effective cluster sampling, elements inside each cluster should be highly heterogeneous (internally diverse) to accurately model the overall population[cite: 8].

15. What is the fundamental definition and trade-off of Non-Random (Non-Probability) Sampling?

Explanation: Non-random sampling exchanges probability statistics for logistical ease, removing the guarantee of full population representativeness[cite: 8].

16. Which non-probability technique relies strictly on immediate subject accessibility and is often used for medical studies requiring voluntary participants?

Explanation: Convenience sampling relies on picking easily accessible, voluntary targets, making it popular in medical clinical baseline recruitment setups[cite: 8].

17. In Judgment Sampling, who acts as the primary filter to select specific items or subjects for the study?

18. What is the overarching structural rule governing Quota Sampling?

Explanation: Quota sampling forces the non-random final sample distribution to match specific population demographic controls (like gender or race ratios)[cite: 8].

19. For which specific scenario is Snowball Sampling explicitly defined and ideally applied?

Explanation: Snowball sampling uses chain-referrals to uncover hidden, hard-to-reach, or rare populations (e.g., marginalized groups or specific expert types)[cite: 8].

20. According to research methodology frameworks, what is the core logistical advantage of using Cluster Random Sampling?

Explanation: Cluster sampling is favored logistically because it allows researchers to study naturally occurring groups, significantly lowering field travel and geographic tracking costs[cite: 8].
